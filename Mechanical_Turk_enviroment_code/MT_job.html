Thank you for assisting with this research. Our goal is to develop methods to automatically extract and map disaster impact information from social media. In this task you will be asked to mark certain words in Twitter tweets to indicate whether they describe disaster impacts, or location information, to create a training set for our methods.

Impacts refer to specific things that have happened as a result of a disaster event (e.g. damage to buildings, death of people or livestock, loss of services such as electricity or communication). You will be provided with examples and instructions on how to identify impacts below.

Please note that the wider disaster event (e.g. earthquake) is NOT considered an impact for our purposes.
Magnitude numbers are also NOT considered as a severity element. We are looking for specific damage or other physical and tangible impacts that have resulted from a disaster event.

Please note that some of the tweets will be reviewed for tagging accuracy.

If you do not speak English fluently, please do not continue, as we are interested in the understanding of fluent English speakers.

You will not be asked for any identifying information, and we will not record your IP address or any other means by which to identify you, and you may exit the task at any time.

Disaster Impact Tags

Type of impact
Here we are looking for text that identifies the type of damage or impact that the disaster has created. Possible examples: malfunction, closure, or physical damage. We are NOT looking for wider disaster events here(e.g earthquake)
Item affected
The item damaged tag should identify the item that has been damaged/affected from the disaster event. Possible examples: people, animals, infrastructure ie buildings or roads, or services (ie power or transport).
Severity/Quantity
This text relates to the scale or amount of the damage impact on the item. Possible examples: duration, number impacted, or reference to complete or partial, or very severe or slight damage that the item has suffered.

Location Information Tags

Place name
Here we are looking for text that describes the location of the impact. This could be a city, town or a smaller location area. We are not looking for the location of other things (not impacts), or for place names that are used metaphorically or comparatively.
Location modifier
Often appearing just before the place name, identify text such as: in, at, near, around, on, outside, etc. which can describe where the location is and what is the relationship of the place name location to the item damaged.
